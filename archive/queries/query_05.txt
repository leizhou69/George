TASK: Identify what distinguishes the repeat loci that cause diseases

BACKGROUND: There are over 6,000 GCN repeats on the 5' UTRs (5UTR) of human genes. Of those, 7 are known to cause genetic disease due to their expansion.
 
GOAL: Understand what the unique characteristics of pathogenic repeats are.


DATA: 
	(1) The B_5UTR_Pathogenic_GCNs.txt file has the 7 pathogenic repeats, their unique id are in the "locus_id" column.
    
    (2) The B_Cat_5UTR_GCNs_masked.tsv file that has all the other GCN repeats in 5UTR region. This file has other fields, including:
		LocusId:	Unique id in the format "chrom-start-end-motif". Same as the "variant_id".
		CanonicalMotif:	The reference repeat motif, normalized by computing all cyclic shifts.
		NumRepeatsInReference: Locus width divided by motif size.
		ReferenceRepeatPurity: Fraction of bases with the locus interval that exactly match perfect repeats of the given motif.
		NsInFlanks: Number of "N" bases in the reference genome within +/-1000bp of the TR locus. 
		TRsInRegion: Number of TRs in the vicinity of this locus that are separated from each other by no more than 6bp of spacer sequence.
		Source:	The source catalog (Table 2) that contributed this locus definition.
		LeftFlankMappability: UCSC 36-mer mappability track per-base mappability scores averaged across a 150bp window of flanking sequence immediately to the left of the TR locus. Range: 0 to 1
		RightFlankMappability: UCSC 36-mer mappability track per-base mappability scores averaged across a 150bp window of flanking sequence immediately to the right of the TR locus. Range: 0 to 1
		FlanksAndLocusMappability: UCSC 36-mer mappability track per-base mappability scores averaged across the TR locus +/- 150bp of flanking sequence. Range: 0 to 1
		VariationCluster: The chrom:start-end of coordinates of the variation cluster that contains this TR locus
		VariationClusterSizeDiff: The difference in size between the original TR locus and the region spanned by the variation cluster. Range: 6 to 8,308 bp
		GencodeGeneRegion: The most significant gene region that this locus overlaps based on Gencode v46 gene annotations. 
		GencodeGeneName: The first gene whose reported GencodeGeneRegion overlaps this locus based on Gencode v46 annotations
		GencodeGeneId: ENSG gene id of the first gene whose reported GencodeGeneRegion overlaps this locus based on Gencode v46 annotations
		GencodeTranscriptId: ENST transcript id of the first transcript whose reported GencodeGeneRegion overlaps this locus based on Gencode v46 annotations
		LPSLengthStdevFromHPRC100: Standard deviation of the number of repeats in the longest pure segment (LPS) detected at this tandem repeat locus in 100 high-coverage long-read (LR) samples from the HPRC. Range: 0 to 123.0
		LPSMotifFractionFromHPRC100: The fraction of alleles at this tandem repeat locus in which the given motif composed the longest pure segment (LPS) among 100 high-coverage long-read (LR) samples from the HPRC.
		AlleleFrequenciesFromIllumina174k: Allele frequencies based on short read data from 2,504 1kGP samples.
		StdevFromIllumina174k: Standard deviation of the allele frequency distribution represented by AlleleFrequenciesFromIllumina174k. Range: 0 to 31.5
		AlleleFrequenciesFromT2TAssemblies: Allele frequencies from analyzing 78 diploid T2T assemblies.
		StdevFromT2TAssemblies: Standard deviation of the allele frequency distribution represented by AlleleFrequenciesFromT2TAssemblies. Range: 0 to 1267.3
	
    (3) A series of .tsv files output by AlphaGenome for the impact of repeat expansion.
	The naming of these series files is as follows.
	"_2xAG" - the consequence of 2 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).
	"_5xAG" - the consequence of 5 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).
	"_20xAG" - the consequence of 20 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).

	The AlphaGenome output files were too large, so they were divided into three files based on the "output_type".
	"_exp" - contains prediction of the expansion effect on RNA_SEQ and CAGE.
	"_splice" - contains output_type related with RNA splicing.
	"_epi" - contains the rest of the output types, mostly have to do with TF binding and epigenetics.

The following is a brief description of all columns in the AlphaGenome prediction columns:


	original_variant_id – identifier for the over 6,000 5 UTR GCN-type repeats
	scored_interval – Genomic window actually used for prediction
	gene_id – Ensembl gene ID
	gene_name – Human-readable gene symbol for the same gene.
	gene_type – Gene biotype (e.g. `protein_coding`, `lncRNA`).
	gene_strand – `+` or `-` strand of that gene on the genome.
	junction_Start, junction_End – Genomic start/end coordinates of the splice junction
	output_type – Modality being scored
	track_name – Identifier for the track (assay + cell type / tissue)
	track_strand – Strand of the track data if applicable (`+`, `-`, or `.` for unstranded).
	Assay – High-level assay type, e.g. `ATAC-seq`, `DNase-seq`, `Histone ChIP-seq`, `polyA plus RNA-seq`, `PRO-cap`
	ontology_curie – Ontology ID pointing to the cell type or tissue
	biosample_name – Human-readable sample name
	biosample_life_stage – Developmental/life-stage descriptor if available (e.g. `adult`, `fetal`; often `NaN` in current dumps)
	data_source – Where the track came from
	transcription_factor – For `CHIP_TF` tracks: name of the TF
	histone_mark – For `CHIP_HISTONE` tracks: mark type
	gtex_tissue – For GTEx RNA-seq tracks: the GTEx tissue label 
	raw_score – The actual numeric effect size for this variant × track × scorer:
		
		- For gene-expression scorers: log fold-change in mean RNA coverage (ALT vs REF) over the gene body.
			
		- For center-mask (ATAC/TF/histone) scorers: log change (often log2 or log1p) in summed signal in the window around the variant.
			
		- For splicing scorers: Δ in splice-site usage, class probabilities, or junction counts, depending on the scorer.  
			Sign gives direction (ALT up/down), magnitude is effect size.
	quantile_score – Position of that `raw_score` in the empirical distribution for that scorer/track:
