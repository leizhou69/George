TASK: Identify what distinguishes the repeat loci that cause diseases

BACKGROUND: There are over 5,000 CCG repeats on the 5' UTRs (5UTR) of human genes. Of those, 7 are known to cause genetic disease due to their expansion.
 
GOAL: Understand what the unique characteristics of pathogenic repeats are.


DATA: 
	(1) The B_5UTR_Pathogenic_CCG.txt file has the 7 pathogenic repeats, their unique id are in the "variant_id" column.

	(2) A series of .tsv files output by AlphaGenome for the impact of repeat expansion.
	The naming of these series files is as follows.
	"_2xAG" - the consequence of 2 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).
	"_5xAG" - the consequence of 5 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).
	"_20xAG" - the consequence of 20 times expansion predicted by AlphaGenome (AG).

	The AlphaGenome output files were too large, so they were divided into three files based on the "output_type".
	"_exp" - contains prediction of the expansion effect on RNA_SEQ and CAGE.
	"_splice" - contains output_type related with RNA splicing.
	"_epi" - contains the rest of the output types, mostly have to do with TF binding and epigenetics.

The following is a brief description of all columns in the AlphaGenome prediction columns:


	original_variant_id – identifier for the over 5,000 5 UTR CCG-type repeats
	scored_interval – Genomic window actually used for prediction
	gene_id – Ensembl gene ID
	gene_name – Human-readable gene symbol for the same gene.
	gene_type – Gene biotype (e.g. `protein_coding`, `lncRNA`).
	gene_strand – `+` or `-` strand of that gene on the genome.
	junction_Start, junction_End – Genomic start/end coordinates of the splice junction
	output_type – Modality being scored
	track_name – Identifier for the track (assay + cell type / tissue)
	track_strand – Strand of the track data if applicable (`+`, `-`, or `.` for unstranded).
	Assay – High-level assay type, e.g. `ATAC-seq`, `DNase-seq`, `Histone ChIP-seq`, `polyA plus RNA-seq`, `PRO-cap`
	ontology_curie – Ontology ID pointing to the cell type or tissue
	biosample_name – Human-readable sample name
	biosample_life_stage – Developmental/life-stage descriptor if available (e.g. `adult`, `fetal`; often `NaN` in current dumps)
	data_source – Where the track came from
	transcription_factor – For `CHIP_TF` tracks: name of the TF
	histone_mark – For `CHIP_HISTONE` tracks: mark type
	gtex_tissue – For GTEx RNA-seq tracks: the GTEx tissue label 
	raw_score – The actual numeric effect size for this variant × track × scorer:
		
		- For gene-expression scorers: log fold-change in mean RNA coverage (ALT vs REF) over the gene body.
			
		- For center-mask (ATAC/TF/histone) scorers: log change (often log2 or log1p) in summed signal in the window around the variant.
			
		- For splicing scorers: Δ in splice-site usage, class probabilities, or junction counts, depending on the scorer.  
			Sign gives direction (ALT up/down), magnitude is effect size.
	quantile_score – Position of that `raw_score` in the empirical distribution for that scorer/track:
